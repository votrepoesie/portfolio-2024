Overview ✨

In this class project, I received a design brief to 1) create a new feature for Bumble and 2) build upon the Google Material Design system. Thus, I discovered user needs that aligned with the business goal and created new components for the design system.

My design focused on creating a design intervention that facilitates organic connections with people you pass by, enhancing the user experience by bridging digital interactions with real-world encounters.

Research ✨

Interpreting Business Goal

Based on the design brief and quantitative data, I interpreted the client's goal is be converting existing, non-paying users of Bumble BFF & BIZZ to subscribe to a new geolocation feature.

Discovering User Needs

After establishing the business goal, I explored how geolocation can create value for users.

I conducted secondary research through online forums, collecting various complaints from Bumble users who used BFF & BIZZ. I used the data to construct three user personas with the 5W1H method, ideating potential solutions that solved specific needs.

Brainstorming with simple one-liner ideas to gauge interest from peers

Design for Authentic Connection

I sought to reframe the problem from converting users to pay for geolocation to creating authentic connections from the advantage of proximity.

Design Process ✨

Art imitates life, or the other way around...

Are you the type of person to strike up a conversation with a stranger?

Do you ever walk past someone on the street and wish you'd be friends with them? Or while you're waiting for the subway? Hanging out at a cafe? Attending a conference?

When you meet people IRL, you don't have time to compose a smart message. You start talking, and it's easy to tell whether you click with each other.

With proximity as source material, Chance Encounter is a new feature that aims to capture the fun and light-hearted nature of spontaneous interactions.

Final prototype demo - keep reading to learn more about why I created these features!

Testing Potential Interactions

In order to facilitate organic interactions, I brainstormed various ways to encourage users to meet up after matching with Chance Encounter.

The problem with all of these ideas was that users don't find them natural.

The key to solving this problem is not to incentivize users to meet up, but to make it fun and exciting for them to do so. Thus, I found gamification to be a viable solution.

Bee-themed Gamification

I was inspired by the idea of exchanging codes in spy movies. This would be a fun way to break the ice and make the interaction more memorable.

New Components for Design System

To create a cohesive experience, I designed new components for the Google Material Design system.

Reflection ✨

The ideation process for this project was a lot of fun, because there was lot of out-of-the-box, despair-inducing, and illuminating experimentations!

Dramatics aside, this project was daunting because I have never worked with a design system before. There was a lot to cover: onboarding and introducing the feature, building out the interactions for the actual feature, as well as differentiating between free and premium features. The best way for me to keep reframing and iterating upon my idea was to receive feedback, then I’d open my sketchbook and quickly work through my logic through mind maps, user flows, and wireframes.

Embrace the unlikely and trust the process.
