Institute on the Formation of Knowledge

From 2021 to 2023, I designed social media and email marketing campaigns following the institute's branding guide, boosting engagement by 34% and showcasing the institute's innovative social sciences research and programs.

Increased the online exposure of new researchers

In order to get more students invested in what the institute was doing and take classes with the postdocs, social media was the best way to reach our audience.

However, academic research can seem cold and unapproachable, not to mention researchers often write about their work with their colleagues in mind. Therefore, I deemed it important to showcase the postdocs using simpler language, visuals, and connect their research to familiar topics.

My social media posts resulted in a notable increase in class size and made the ongoing research projects more memorable not only to outsiders, but also made it easier for staff members to communicate with the postdocs.

Art Museum, Chinese University of Hong Kong

As a summer intern in the marketing department, I was tasked with creating a digital magazine to promote the museum's then-incoming exhibition, engaging online viewers by linking art historical research with contemporary issues.

To do this, I conducted 50+ hours of archival research and interpreted academic writings into accesible language and visuals, grouping them under familiar topics such as fashion, interior decor, and travel.
